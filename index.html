Limerick Institute of Technology: H. Dip in Computing in Creative and Multimedia Programming

About LIT & H. Dip Course

The H.Dip in computing in Creative and Multimedia Programming is run at LIT as a Springboard course. LIT is one of Ireland's most dynamic and vibrant educational institutions. It encompasses a wide variety of programmes and people exploring new ideas, knolwedge, art, workshops, studios and labs. LIT offers a warm, friendly atmosphere with approachable and friendly staff to assist students in reaching their full potential. The college has an excellent graduate employment rate with over 90% of graduates in employment or further study. Springboard is a Department of Education and skills initiative that provides a large number of opportunities in higher education courses, leading to qualifications in areas where there are employment opportunities in the economy. Programmes are available at all higher education levels from Certificate to Master's Degree level (levels 6-9 on NFQ). This H.Dip in Creative Multimedia is a level 8 Course that embraces primarily the areas of Computer Programming and Graphic Design.

Course Description

This postgraduate programme offers rewarding and challenging possibilities for learners interested in undertaking a conversion programme in the area of multimedia, and its application in social, media and enterprise environments. Given the scale of the IT sector in Ireland, and the current expansion of roles associated with the IT sector, it is projected that there will be healthy demand for talented Computing/ IT graduates over the next number of years. The aim of this 1 year conversion programme is to provide students with an academically challenging educational experience that will enable them to develop the knowledge base, professional attitudes, project management skills and industry awareness to serve both the multimedia computing industry and society. The course also aims to provide the student with the opportunity to engage in independent research that integrates concepts, issues and ethical principles that allow the student to question existing precepts.

The following modules will be covered:

Semester 1

Web Programming, Web Development, Interactive Multimedia Design, Multimedia Digital Video, 3D Graphics, Multimedia Database Systems & Design.

Semester 2

Semester 3

3 month Work Placement.

Web Development

Web Developent is a module of the course H.Dip in Computing in Creative and Multimedia Programming. This module is designed to provide learner's with an understanding of web development technologies and the skills required to develop websites with dynamic content that conforms to W3C standards. On completion of this course the learner will/should be able to:

  1. Describe the structure and operation of the World Wide Web
  2. Develop HTML Web Pages.
  3. Apply Cascading Style Sheets (CSS) to web pages in order to separate form from content.
  4. Use an authorising package to develop web pages that conform to W3C standards and accessibility guidelines.
  5. Use JavaScript to add dynamic content to web pages.
  6. Publish and maintain a website.
  7. Critically assess website design and accessibility.
